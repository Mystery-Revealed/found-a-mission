# "Found a Mission" — Build Specification
### Unit 2 Game · Spanish Colonial · *Common Build Standards apply (Pattern A — Engine Game)*

## 1. At a Glance
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **TEKS** | 7.2C (missions, presidios, Massanet/Margil/Hidalgo), 7.2B (French threat), 7.9A |
| **Role** | A Franciscan founding an East Texas mission, 1690–1731 (everyone plays the same friar — class-wide group) |
| **Type** | Solo building/management game — 6 phases × 2 decisions = **12 graded actions**; light build-status panel instead of a map |
| **Adapter** | `foundAMission.js` · meters `{ mission, trust, supplies }` start 50 — **trust** = relations with the Caddo, the game's heart |

**Pitch:** Spain has given you a wagon, a bell, and a blessing — build a mission in the Piney Woods among the powerful Caddo, keep it alive through hunger, sickness, and a French war, and learn why some missions took root while others had to move.

**Teaching idea:** missions had **two jobs** (spread the faith, hold the land — your outline's exact framing), and they succeeded or failed mostly on **relations with Native peoples**. The Caddo were a strong, organized farming nation who welcomed trade but largely declined conversion — the game respects that truth: **trust is won by service and respect, never by force**, and even a well-run East Texas mission historically relocated toward San Antonio (1731). "Losing" East Texas while scoring high accuracy = understanding the real history.

## 2. Content Bank
**Fray Damián Massanet** co-founds **San Francisco de los Tejas** (1690), the first East Texas mission, answering the La Salle scare · the **Caddo ("Tejas" = friends)**: settled farmers with their own faith and leaders — hospitable, independent-minded · European **sickness** devastates villages (handle honestly: the friars' presence brought disease they didn't understand; the Caddo connected the deaths to the strangers) · supply lines from Mexico were months long; crops and cattle decided survival · **presidios** protected missions (and sometimes caused friction) · **Francisco Hidalgo's** letter to the French spurred re-entry (1716); **Antonio Margil de Jesús** founded East Texas missions and later the great **San José** at San Antonio · the **Chicken War (1719)**: a small French raid emptied East Texas missions for two years · by **1731** three East Texas missions moved to the San Antonio River — where water, protection, and supply lines were better.

## 3. Mechanics
Meters: **Mission** ⛪ (buildings, fields, herd), **Trust** 🤝 (Caddo relations), **Supplies** 📦. A small status panel shows the mission growing (chapel → fields → acequia → herd). Strength check: if **Trust** hits 0 the mission fails early (the real failure mode). Ending = sum → tiers ("A Mission That Took Root" / "The Bell Still Rings" / "The Woods Reclaim It") + debrief: the 1731 move to San Antonio and why the river missions thrived (the bridge to San Antonio's founding in Claim the Land).

## 4. Sample Scripted Phase + Beat Matrix
**Phase 1 — Choosing Ground (decision 1):** *Where do you build?*
- ✅ **Near the Caddo villages and a good creek — close enough to serve, far enough to respect.** Mission +10, Trust +10. *"Massanet built among the Tejas, whose own name meant 'friends.' Water and neighbors were the whole plan."*
- ⚠️ **Deep in the woods, apart from everyone.** Mission +5, Trust −5. *"Peaceful — and pointless. A mission with no neighbors serves no one."*
- ❌ **On the Caddo's own planted fields — the best soil.** Trust −15. *"Taking planted ground makes enemies of the friends you came to find."*

**Phase 1 (decision 2):** *First meeting with the Caddo leaders.* ✅ Bring gifts, learn names, ask permission (Trust +15) · ⚠️ Preach first, talk later (Trust −5, Mission +5) · ❌ Read a proclamation claiming the land for Spain (Trust −15).

**Beat matrix:**
| Phase | Beat | Right answer teaches |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | Building year | Plant food crops before finishing the chapel walls (✅ survival first); teach by serving — the forge, the cattle, the garden (✅ how missions actually drew interest) |
| 3 | The sickness (sensitive) | Nurse the sick openly and grieve with the villages (✅ compassion; feedback honestly notes the disease came with the strangers and trust was terribly strained); never blame the Caddo's own healers (❌ option) |
| 4 | Hunger winter | Share mission stores with hungry neighbors (✅ — service builds what sermons can't); beg the slow supply line from Mexico (⚠️) |
| 5 | The Chicken War (1719) | Evacuate calmly with records and the herd when the French raid comes (✅ what missions really did); stand siege with no soldiers (❌) |
| 6 | The decision (1731) | Accept the move to the San Antonio River — water, presidio, supply road (✅ the historical choice); the debrief honors what was learned in the woods |

## 5. Higgsfield Assets (standard direction + dignity rules)
1 Title: "A small log-and-thatch mission with a wooden bell frame in a clearing among giant pines, morning mist." · 2 First meeting: "A friar with gifts meeting Caddo leaders before their great grass houses — mutual dignity." · 3 Building: "Friars and Caddo neighbors raising a timber chapel wall together, fields sprouting." · 4 The hard year: "A candlelit sickroom, a friar and a Caddo healer both tending — sorrow and care, no despair-porn." · 5 Chicken War: "A hurried peaceful evacuation, carts and cattle on a forest road at dawn." · 6 Ending: "A stone mission rising by a green river (San Antonio), the old wooden bell hanging in the new tower."

## 6. Command Center & Notes
Class-wide accuracy; PDF footer TEKS 7.2C, 7.2B. **Sensitivity:** the missions' legacy is complicated — the game grades **respect, service, and honesty**, never forced conversion (force options always score wrong with plain feedback). The Caddo keep their agency: they choose trade and friendship on their terms, and mostly kept their own faith — say so. The epidemic phase is the hardest beat: honest at headline level ("the strangers' sickness"), grief shared, no blame on the Caddo.
